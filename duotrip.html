Duolingo makes language learning fun and accessible, but it doesn’t always reflect how people use language, especially when preparing for travel. DuoTrip is a new mode designed to bridge that gap, helping users turn their travel plans into practical language preparation. Through this project, I designed a system that aligns lessons with real-world scenarios, enabling learners to feel confident navigating conversations abroad.

At a Glance

How can you prepare to communicate in another language while traveling?

When traveling abroad, many people face uncertainty and anxiety around communicating in a foreign language. Even with access to tools like Duolingo, learners often struggle to transition from lessons to real-world conversations. Traditional language learning platforms tend to prioritize vocabulary and grammar over practical speaking confidence, leaving users underprepared for spontaneous interactions.

User Quote
Solution

DuoTrip is Duolingo’s newest solution to help travelers prepare for the exact moments they’ll face abroad.

Research

Our target learner: people who want to connect, not just translate.

Inspired by my friend, I identified the target learner as self-motivated individuals. self-motivated individuals who are genuinely interested in learning a new language as a way to connect more deeply with others. Rather than viewing language as a transactional tool, they see it as a bridge to culture, relationships, and authentic experiences.

They value meaningful interaction, are driven by curiosity, and are willing to put in consistent effort, but need better support in building confidence and applying their skills in real-world contexts.

User Journey

What does the current learning experience look like for travelers?

To prepare for the upcoming trip, Brandon suggested they download Duolingo and learn together. At first, it worked well—the app’s gamified features kept everyone motivated and engaged.

Over time, however, Brandon began to question whether the lessons he was practicing were truly relevant to his trip. Certain phrases appeared frequently but didn’t seem applicable to real travel situations. Reflecting on the experience afterward, he realized he wished the lessons had been more closely aligned with the situations he actually encountered.

Desk Research

Across forums and user discussions online, similar feedback appears again and again.

These issues aren't just something we heard in one case. Users report that they have limited control over what they learn, that some lessons feel disconnected from real travel scenarios, and that while Duolingo provides a lot of vocabulary, it doesn’t always prioritize the vocabulary travelers need most.

Online Sentiment

This led to the formation of this project's problem statement.

Conceptualize

Visualizing the solution over the span of the pre-trip process.

To explore how the solution could come to life, I created a simple storyboard that visualizes the user experience across key moments of the journey. The tangible narrative showed how a traveler might interact with the new feature before going on their trip.

Prototype

Understanding and following Duolingo's design language for the new feature.

Starting with low-fidelity wireframes, I focused on quickly exploring core functionality without getting distracted by visual details. This allowed me to iterate rapidly, test different ideas, and prioritize the key features that support real-world language practice.

As the direction became clearer, I transitioned into mid-fidelity designs, refining the interaction patterns. At this stage, I began to introduce more realistic content and clearer affordances, ensuring the experience felt intuitive and aligned with user expectations.

Reflection

How to fit a new featuring into an existing ecosystem.

This project reinforced the importance of designing new features with the existing ecosystem in mind. Rather than treating the solution as a standalone idea, I had to consider how it would integrate seamlessly into a platform like Duolingo, aligning with its current user experience.

Introducing a new feature isn’t just about solving a problem; it’s about ensuring it feels cohesive and additive rather than disruptive. This required a deeper understanding of how users already engage with the product, and how small shifts in functionality can impact the broader journey.

Ultimately, this project highlighted that successful design is not only about innovation, but also about thoughtful integration.
